# **2\. The Concept & Core Gameplay Loop**

SlitherBet takes the simple, universally understood mechanics of snake/slither games and layers real-money stakes on top. The result is a product that requires no learning curve for the game mechanics, yet demands genuine skill in strategy, resource management, and risk/reward decision-making.

## **2.1 The Arena**

Each match takes place in a bounded circular arena. All 100 players enter simultaneously at random spawn points. The arena contains thousands of glowing dots scattered across the floor — each dot is a visual representation of real currency.

## **2.2 How the Money Works**

| The Dot Economy Each dot in the arena \= a fractional share of the total prize pool. When 100 players buy in at $10 each, the $1,000 pot (minus rake) is distributed as collectible dots across the arena. Eating dots grows your snake AND increases your claim on the prize pool. |
| :---- |

When a snake is eliminated (head hits another snake's body), all the dots it has accumulated are instantly released back into the arena — creating a feeding frenzy mechanic identical to coins dropping in a battle royale. Surviving players fight over the windfall.

# **3\. Advanced Game Mechanics & Skill Elements**

One of SlitherBet's key regulatory and market advantages is the arguable skill component built into gameplay. This positions it more favorably in jurisdictions that distinguish skill-based from pure chance games.

## **3.1 Core Skill Elements**

* **Navigation:** Collision avoidance — reading opponent trajectories requires spatial reasoning and reaction speed

* **Encirclement:** Coiling trap strategy — circling opponents to force them into your body is an advanced tactical move

* **Foraging:** Dot prioritization — choosing which deposits to chase vs. risk assessments is a real-money decision

* **Risk/Reward:** Size management — a larger snake is more powerful but slower and harder to maneuver, creating risk tradeoffs

* **Timing:** Predator/prey switching — knowing when to hunt vs. flee based on relative size

## **3.2 Power-Ups & Special Events (Optional Layer)**

To increase engagement and monetization opportunities, the arena can include timed special events:

* Speed Boost Orbs — temporary acceleration, high risk/high reward for aggressive plays

* Magnet Dots — briefly attract nearby dot clusters to your snake

* Fog Zones — sections of arena become temporarily obscured, pure chaos events

* Double-Value Dots — rare gold dots worth 2x, triggering high-competition scrambles

* Safe Zone Shrink — in late-game, arena slowly contracts, forcing confrontation (battle royale mechanic)

# **4\. Business Model & Revenue Streams**

## **4.1 Primary Revenue: House Rake**

The house charges a rake (commission) on every pot. This is taken from buy-ins before dots are distributed, so the prize pool players compete for is always the net-of-rake amount.

| Tier | Rake % | House Take Per Seat |
| :---: | :---: | :---: |
| **Micro ($0.50)** | 10% | $0.05 per player |
| **Standard ($5)** | 8% | $0.40 per player |
| **Mid-Stakes ($25)** | 6% | $1.50 per player |
| **High-Stakes ($100)** | 5% | $5.00 per player |
| **VIP ($500)** | 4% | $20.00 per player |

| Revenue Model Advantage: Unlike slots or casino table games, the house is not on the losing side of any bet. The rake is extracted regardless of who wins, creating a stable, predictable revenue stream that scales linearly with volume. |
| :---- |

## **4.2 Secondary Revenue Streams**

* Premium Snake Skins & Cosmetics — no gameplay advantage, purely aesthetic. Industry data shows 20–30% of engaged players purchase cosmetics.

* Spectator Mode Subscriptions — players can watch high-stakes lobbies in real time for a monthly fee ($4.99/mo). Creates a Twitch-adjacent experience.

* SlitherBet Pass (Battle Pass) — monthly/seasonal progression system with cosmetic rewards for logged gameplay hours. $9.99/month.

* Affiliate & Referral Program — tracked referral links with revenue share, leveraging the gaming influencer ecosystem.

* API White-Label Licensing — license the engine to other gambling operators for B2B revenue in Year 3+.

# **6\. Regulatory Framework & Licensing**

## **6.2 Target Licensing Jurisdictions (Phase 1\)**

| Jurisdiction | Notes |
| :---- | :---- |
| **UK Gambling Commission** | Gold-standard license. Expensive (\~£50–100K setup \+ ongoing fees) but gives access to the UK market and credibility globally. Strict AML/KYC requirements. |
| **Malta Gaming Authority (MGA)** | Most respected EU license. Required for EU player access. B2B and B2C licenses available. \~€25K setup \+ annual fees. |
| **Isle of Man** | Respected, English-law jurisdiction. Lower cost than UKGC/MGA. Good for initial operations while building track record. |
| **Curaçao** | Quick to obtain, low cost (\~$25K/year). Limited credibility. Use only as a bridge license while pursuing Tier 1 licenses. |

## **6.3 Compliance Infrastructure**

* KYC/AML — Age verification (18+), identity verification, source of funds checks at higher deposit levels

* Responsible Gambling Tools — Mandatory deposit limits, self-exclusion, cooling-off periods, reality checks

* Geofencing — Hard IP and GPS blocks on unlicensed territories (US, most of Asia)

* Data Protection — GDPR compliance for EU users; designated DPO required

* Tax Compliance — Point of Consumption taxes (21% in UK), varied by jurisdiction

* Monthly RNG Auditing — Independent testing lab (eCOGRA, GLI) to certify fairness
